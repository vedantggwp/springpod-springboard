### Brand Personality Archetype

**Primary: The Guide** -- Springpod positions itself as the knowledgeable, supportive companion that helps young people navigate career decisions and helps organisations build talent pipelines. It does not lecture (Authority) or disrupt (Rebel); it walks alongside.

**Secondary: The Innovator** -- Particularly on the B2B side, with language around "AI-powered Interview Coach," "Virtual-to-Reality" programmes, and platform-scale thinking ("One membership. All your outcomes.").

**Tertiary: The Friend** -- On student-facing copy, the tone is warm, encouraging, and peer-like ("Let's go," emoji use, direct "you" address).

### Vocabulary Patterns

**Words They Use Frequently:**
- "Experience" / "experiences" (core product language)
- "Impact" / "social impact" / "social value"
- "Diverse" / "diversity" / "inclusive"
- "Pipeline" / "talent pipeline"
- "Career-connected"
- "Work-ready" / "workplace readiness"
- "Outcomes" / "results"
- "Scale" / "at scale"
- "Boost" (as both noun and verb)
- "Confidence" / "confident"
- "Enrolment" / "learner"
- "Bridge the gap"
- "Futures" / "future" / "build futures"
- "Real-world" / "real careers"
- "Free" / "100% free" (student-facing)
- "Meaningful"
- "Mission"

**Words They Avoid:**
- "Internship" (they use "micro-internship" or "work experience" instead)
- "Training" (too corporate/didactic; they prefer "experience" or "programme")
- "Cheap" / "budget" (they say "free" or focus on ROI)
- "Student" in B2B context (they prefer "young people" or "learners")
- "Product" in student context (they prefer "experience" or "programme")
- Heavy jargon without context
- Negative framing (problems are acknowledged briefly, then reframed as opportunities)
- "Job board" or transactional employment language

### UI Component Patterns

**Buttons:**
- Primary: Teal (`#0BB3B7`) background, white text, rounded corners
- Student CTAs use informal labels ("Let's go," "Find experiences")
- B2B CTAs use value-driven labels ("Calculate your impact," "Talk to us")

**Cards:**
- Light gray (`#E4ECF7`) backgrounds on white pages
- Statistics presented in grid cards with large numbers and supporting descriptions
- Product cards group features with check-mark lists

**Hero Sections:**
- Student site: Full-bleed imagery with overlaid text, emoji-enhanced headlines
- B2B site: Clean, text-forward heroes with navy backgrounds and teal accents
- Statistics bars with multiple metrics displayed horizontally

**Navigation:**
- Student site: Minimal, flat navigation
- B2B site: Mega-menu with categorised dropdowns (Experiences > Study/Work/Skills, Solutions > Employers/HE/Government)
- Consistent "Talk to us" / "Get in touch" persistent CTA

**Statistics/Social Proof:**
- Large, bold numbers with supporting context
- Format: "45,000+" with "Monthly active young people" beneath
- Currency format for financial impact: "£98m Social value created"
- Percentage format for outcomes: "59% Boost in workplace readiness"

### Spacing & Layout

- B2B site uses generous whitespace with clear section breaks
- Student site is denser with more visual elements and imagery
- Content sections follow a consistent rhythm: headline > supporting copy > CTA > visual proof
- Grid layouts for product cards (3-column on desktop)

### Icon Style

- Lucide-style line icons on the documentation/framework side
- Custom SVG illustrations on both student and B2B sites
- Decorative SVG shapes (curved lines, dots) used as background elements, particularly in teal and accent red

### Image & Photography Style

- Student site: Young people in workplace/educational settings, aspirational but authentic
- B2B site: More abstract, using illustrations and data visualisations over photography
- Employer logos displayed as trust signals on B2B property

### Animation & Motion

- Subtle entrance animations on scroll (fade-in/slide-up)
- No heavy animation or parallax effects
- Cookie consent and modal overlays use standard transitions
- Interactive elements (buttons, links) use hover state colour transitions

## Writing Guidelines

### Do

- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Use "experience" (not "course," "module," or "training") as the core product noun
- Address young people directly with "you/your"
- Acknowledge uncertainty and anxiety before offering solutions
- Back B2B claims with specific numbers (percentages, pound figures)
- Use active voice and action verbs ("Gain," "Build," "Unlock," "Earn")
- Keep sentences short -- aim for 15-20 words average
- Use sentence case for headings (not Title Case, except proper nouns)
- Emojis are permitted on student-facing content, used sparingly in headlines
- Frame diversity and inclusion as core to the mission, not an add-on

### Don't

- Use education jargon without context (if "SROI" appears, explain it)
- Say "students" in B2B copy -- use "young people" or "learners"
- Use passive voice in CTAs ("Get started" not "Getting started is easy")
- Lead with the problem without quickly offering the solution
- Use "cheap," "budget," or "affordable" -- the student product is "free" or "100% free"
- Over-promise outcomes -- always tie claims to data
- Use corporate buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "paradigm")
- Use emojis in B2B or formal communications
- Write CTAs longer than 4 words where possible
- Use fear-based messaging -- the tone should always be empowering

### CTA Conventions

| Audience | Primary CTA Style | Examples |
|----------|-------------------|----------|
| Students | Informal, action verb | "Let's go" / "Find experiences" / "Find out more" |
| B2B | Value proposition | "Calculate your impact" / "Talk to us" / "See our case studies" |
| Blog | Educational | "Get Started" / "Read more" |
| Careers | Community | "Connect" |

### Number & Data Formatting

- Large numbers: Use "+" suffix and comma separators ("45,000+")
- Financial: Use "£" prefix with unit suffix ("£98m," "£19 ROI per £1 invested")
- Percentages: Number + "%" with descriptive label ("59% Boost in workplace readiness")
- Statistics are a first-class content element -- display prominently, not buried in body copy

## Competitor Voice Comparison

### Springpod vs. InvestIN Education

| Dimension | Springpod | InvestIN |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Formality | 4/10 (casual-leaning) | 6/10 (semi-formal, prestige-oriented) |
| Personality | The Guide / Friend | The Authority / Curator |
| Price positioning | "100% free" -- accessibility-first | Premium programmes, certification value |
| Language | "Real-world tasks," "find the right career" | "World-class," "top professionals," "ultimate experience" |
| Social proof | Scale metrics (1m+ enrolments, £98m value) | Prestige markers (UCL, LSE, 70,000+ students from 100+ countries) |
| Audience framing | Empowerment ("figure out the right role for you") | Aspiration ("immersive career experience") |
| Key difference | Springpod is egalitarian and scale-focused; InvestIN is exclusive and prestige-focused |

### Springpod vs. Barclays LifeSkills

| Dimension | Springpod | Barclays LifeSkills |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Formality | 4/10 | 5/10 (conversational yet corporate-backed) |
| Personality | The Guide / Innovator | The Enabler / Supporter |
| Scope | Career experiences + employer pipeline | Employability skills + financial literacy |
| Language | "Experiences," "career-connected," "impact" | "Skills," "confidence," "prepare," "develop" |
| Model | Platform (connecting employers with young people) | Content library (tools and lesson plans) |
| Tone difference | More energetic and bold | More measured and facilitative |
| Key difference | Springpod is a marketplace connecting talent to employers; LifeSkills is a resource library backed by a bank brand |

### Springpod vs. Speakers for Schools (now Futures for All)

| Dimension | Springpod | Futures for All |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Formality | 4/10 | 7/10 (mission-driven formal) |
| Personality | The Guide / Innovator | The Advocate / Institution |
| Positioning | Platform-first, tech-enabled | Network-first, relationship-driven |
| Language | "At scale," "membership," "pods" | Accessibility, transparency, social mission |
| Digital maturity | Product-led (pods, dashboards, AI coach) | Organisation-led (speakers network, placements) |
| Key difference | Springpod leads with platform and data; Futures for All leads with network and mission credibility |

### Competitive Voice Summary

Springpod occupies a unique position in the UK edtech/work experience space: **more accessible than InvestIN, more energetic than Barclays LifeSkills, and more tech-forward than Futures for All**. Its core differentiator in voice is the combination of warmth (student-facing) with data confidence (B2B-facing), underpinned by a platform-scale narrative that none of its competitors match.
