# Roles and Contacts

This document defines who does what in SpringBoard — who reviews builds, who approves tools, and who to contact when something goes wrong.

---

## Framework roles

| Role | Person | Contact | Responsibilities |
|------|--------|---------|------------------|
| **Peer reviewer** | Any team member | — | Reviews Yellow builds. Checks that the builder followed the checklist and the tool works as described. |
| **Technical reviewer** | Engineering Lead (Product & Engineering tribe) | tech-review@springpod.com | Reviews Orange builds. Checks security, data handling, access controls, and technical architecture. |
| **Designated reviewer** | Oliver Fisher (CPO) or delegate | designated-review@springpod.com | Signs off Red builds. Verifies compliance, privacy impact, and age-appropriate design. Has authority to approve or block launch. |
| **Framework contact** | SpringBoard Admin | springboard@springpod.com | Answers questions about SpringBoard itself. Updates framework documents. Runs training sessions. |
| **Emergency contact** | On-call Engineering | emergency@springpod.com / Slack: #incident-response | First call for critical incidents (data exposure, safety risk, compliance breach). Available outside business hours. |

---

## Who reviews what

| Build path | Reviewer role | What they check |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Green** (Quick Build) | No formal review required | Builder self-checks using the [Green Checklist](/checklists/green-checklist) |
| **Yellow** (Standard Build) | Peer reviewer | Checklist completion, basic functionality, realistic data testing |
| **Orange** (Reviewed Build) | Technical reviewer | Security, data handling, branding, access controls, third-party services |
| **Red** (Protected Build) | Designated reviewer | Everything above, plus compliance, privacy impact, age-appropriate design, incident response plan |

<Collapsible variant="why" title="WHY different reviewers for different paths">

Green builds are low-risk — a self-check is enough. As risk increases, you need reviewers with more expertise. A peer can check if a tool works correctly. A technical reviewer can check if it is secure. A designated reviewer can check if it is compliant. Matching reviewer expertise to risk level keeps the process efficient without cutting corners.

</Collapsible>

## Escalation path

When you are not sure what to do, follow this path:

<DecisionFlow>
  <DecisionStep
    question="Can you solve it yourself using the framework docs?"
    yes="Solve it and document in your Build Log"
    no="Continue"
  />
  <DecisionStep
    question="Is it a question about how to build something?"
    yes="Ask the Framework contact"
    no="Continue"
  />
  <DecisionStep
    question="Is it a security or data concern?"
    yes="Contact the Technical reviewer"
    no="Continue"
  />
  <DecisionStep
    question="Is it a compliance, legal, or safety issue?"
    yes="Contact the Designated reviewer"
    no="Continue"
  />
  <DecisionStep
    question="Is something broken in a live tool?"
    yes="Contact the Emergency contact and file an Incident Report"
    isLast
  />
</DecisionFlow>

See [When to Escalate](/guides/when-to-escalate) for more detailed guidance.
