# Company Context

This document captures Springpod's values, constraints, and policies that shape how we build AI tools.

---

## About Springpod

**Company name:** Springpod

**Industry:** EdTech — virtual work experience and careers education platform

**Size:** ~80 employees

**Mission:** Enable all young people to select and secure their brightest future by democratising access to quality work experience and careers education

**Founded by:** Sam Hyams (CEO) and Oliver Fisher (Co-Founder & CPO), London

**Scale:** 1M+ student enrolments, 4,000+ partner schools, 300+ employer and university partners

**Primary AI use cases:** Internal dashboards, student engagement tools, content generation, employer reporting, AI-powered interview skills coach, automated assessment aids, curriculum content tools

---

## Company values that affect how we build

These principles guide our work and are referenced throughout the framework when making build decisions.

1. Student safety and wellbeing come before speed of delivery
2. Democratise access — remove barriers of geography, cost, and connections
3. Measurable impact — every feature must demonstrate real outcomes
4. Move fast on internal tools, carefully on anything student-facing (ages 13+)
5. Data minimisation — collect only what we strictly need

---

## Constraints

These are the hard limits on what we can build and how we can build it.

1. All student data must comply with UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018
2. No tools that process under-18 data may use unapproved third-party services
3. Student-facing tools must meet WCAG AA accessibility standards
4. No behavioural profiling or recommendation algorithms on student data without explicit review
5. All tools must work on school-issued devices (Chromebooks, iPads, locked-down browsers)

## Compliance requirements

| Regulation | Applies? | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| UK GDPR | Yes | All personal data processing, ICO registered (ZA195343) |
| COPPA (US children's privacy) | Monitoring | US expansion underway, will apply when serving US students under 13 |
| FERPA (US student records) | Not yet | Will apply with US school partnerships |
| CCPA (California privacy) | Not yet | Will apply with California users |
| Data Protection Act 2018 | Yes | Primary UK legislation |
| PECR | Yes | Electronic communications to students and parents |
| Gatsby Benchmarks | Yes | Platform aligned to 8 Gatsby career education benchmarks |

---

## AI policy

- AI-assisted development is encouraged for all build paths
- AI-generated content that students see must be human-reviewed before publication
- The AI-Powered Interview Skills Coach is an approved student-facing AI feature
- AI models processing student data must be approved by the Data team
- Student-facing AI features must disclose AI involvement with age-appropriate language (e.g., "This feedback was created with the help of AI")
- No AI-generated content may make promises about career outcomes

## Data classification

| Classification | Description | Example | Build path |
|---------------|-------------|---------|------------|
| Public | No restriction | Marketing content, blog posts, public programme listings | Green |
| Internal | Business use only | Staff directories, internal metrics, employer pipeline data, team dashboards | Yellow |
| Confidential | Restricted access | Financial data, HR records, partnership agreements, employer contracts | Orange |
| Protected | Highest protection | Student personal data, under-18 data, school records, health/SEND information | Red |

## Keeping this current

This document should be reviewed at least once a year or whenever company policies, compliance requirements, or AI practices change. Key triggers for an update include:

- New regulatory requirements (e.g., entering the US market triggers COPPA/FERPA obligations)
- Changes to the approved AI model list or data processing agreements
- Updates to the Springpod data classification scheme
- New student-facing AI features being approved or retired
- Changes to the ICO registration or data protection impact assessments
