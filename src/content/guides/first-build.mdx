# Your First Build

This walkthrough takes you through building a simple tool using the Vibe Coding Framework from start to finish. By the end, you will have a working tool and know how the framework works.

**What you will build:** A team directory page — a simple web page showing your team members' names, roles, and contact information.

**Time:** About 30-60 minutes total, including the framework steps.

---

## Step 1: Classify your project (3 min)

Open the [Project Intake](/intake/project-intake) and answer the 5 questions. For our team directory, the answers would be:

> **Q1: Who will use this tool?** → Just me or my immediate team (internal)
>
> **Q2: What kind of data will this tool handle?** → Non-sensitive internal data (team names, roles, emails)
>
> **Q3: What happens if this tool breaks or gives wrong information?** → Minor inconvenience — we can work around it
>
> **Q4: How long will this tool be used?** → A few weeks/months, or I am not sure
>
> **Q5: Does this tool use AI to generate content that users see?** → No

**Result:** All answers point to **Green: Quick Build**. That is your build path.

---

## Step 2: Understand your build path (2 min)

The intake told you this is a **Green: Quick Build**. That means:

- Low risk — internal users, no sensitive data
- No formal review required — you self-check using a short checklist
- You can use any AI coding tool you are comfortable with

The framework has four paths (Green → Yellow → Orange → Red), each adding more safeguards. Green is the simplest. You will only need the others when your tool handles more sensitive data or serves more critical audiences.

---

## Step 3: Fill out the Project Brief (5 min)

Open the [Project Brief](/forms/project-brief) and fill it in. For our team directory:

> **Project name:** Team Directory
>
> **Builder:** [Your name]
>
> **Date:** [Today's date]
>
> **Build path:** Green (Quick Build)
>
> **What are you building?** A simple web page that shows everyone on our team — their name, role, and contact info. Used by new joiners to find who does what.
>
> **What problem does it solve?** New team members ask "who handles X?" several times a week. A directory answers this instantly.
>
> **What does "done" look like?**
> 1. Shows all team members with name, role, and email
> 2. Searchable by name or role
> 3. Works on mobile
>
> **What tools will you use?** Lovable (fast for simple pages, no backend needed)

---

## Step 4: Build it (15-30 min)

Open your AI coding tool and describe what you want. Here is a prompt you can adapt:

```
Create a team directory page that shows team members in a card layout.
Each card shows: name, role, and email address.
Include a search bar at the top that filters by name or role.
Make it responsive so it works on mobile.
Use these colors: primary #1B3A5C, background #FFFFFF, text #333333.
Do not use any external libraries. Keep it simple.
Do not add any features I did not ask for.
```

**Tips:**

- If the first result is not right, tell the AI what to change. Be specific: "Make the cards wider" is better than "fix the layout."
- If something is broken, tell the AI what you expected versus what happened.
- You do not need to understand the code. You need to verify that it works.

---

## Step 5: Test it (5 min)

Try using the tool the way a real person would:

1. **Search for someone** — does the search work?
2. **Search for something that does not exist** — does it show a clear "no results" message?
3. **Open it on your phone** — does it still work?
4. **Try typing special characters** in the search (accents, apostrophes) — does it handle them?

If anything is broken, tell the AI tool and ask it to fix it.

---

## Step 6: Check for secrets (2 min)

Search your code for any of these: `sk-`, `api_key`, `secret`, `password`, `token`

For a simple team directory, you probably will not have any. But check anyway — it is a habit worth building.

If you find any, move them to your tool's environment variables (usually in Settings or Secrets).

---

## Step 7: Ship it (2 min)

1. **Get the URL** from your AI tool (most provide one automatically)
2. **Know the kill switch** — how would you take this offline? For most tools: set it to private or delete the deployment
3. **Share the link** with your team
