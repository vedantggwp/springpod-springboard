# Build Log

Keep a running log of decisions and changes during your build. This is your project diary — when someone asks "why did you do it that way?", this log has the answer.

Required for Yellow+ builds. Recommended for all builds.

## How to use this log

Copy the entry template below for each work session. Date each entry. Focus on decisions and problems, not just what you did.

<Collapsible variant="why" title="WHY keep a build log">

Three reasons:

1. **Future you** — in two months, you will not remember why you chose Chart.js over D3, or why the dashboard has 4 tabs instead of 5.
2. **Handover** — if someone else needs to modify your tool, the build log tells them what you tried, what worked, and what did not.
3. **Prompt preservation** — AI prompts that produced good results are valuable. Save them here so you (or others) can reuse them.

</Collapsible>

## Entry template

Copy everything below the line for each new entry.

---

### Date: ___

**What I did:**

[Brief description of work done this session]

**Decisions made:**

[Any choices you made and why. Be specific.]

> Example: "Used Chart.js instead of D3 because Chart.js was simpler for basic line charts and had better documentation for beginners."

**AI prompts used:**

[Paste or summarize the prompts you gave the AI tool. Include the tool name.]

> Example: "Replit Agent — 'Create a line chart showing daily attendance percentage for the last 30 days. Use Chart.js. Data comes from the /api/attendance endpoint. Use the company colors: primary #1B3A5C, accent #2ABFBF.'"

**Issues encountered:**

[Anything that went wrong, surprised you, or took longer than expected]

**Next steps:**

[What you plan to do in the next session]

---

## Tips for effective entries

- **Be honest about problems.** "The AI generated code that crashed when the database was empty" is useful. "Everything went fine" is not.
- **Save your prompts.** Especially the ones that worked well. They save time when building similar features later.
- **Note what you changed from AI output.** If the AI generated something and you modified it, record what you changed and why.
- **Keep entries short.** A few sentences per section is enough. This is a log, not an essay.
